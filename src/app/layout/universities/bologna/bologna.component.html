The oldest university in the world

      Founded in 1088, Bologna University is the oldest university in the world and it has been student-centered whilst attracting prominent figures from science and the arts. Today it is a leader in the European Higher Education Area and famous for its beauty and integration with the city. Its teaching catalogue is diversified and tailored to the needs of present-day society: 221 degree programs of which 78 international and 62 taught in English, 33 Departments and over 87,000 students. A further 4.000 are candidates for its PhDs and 3rd cycle programs. The caliber of its graduates, its internationally renowned teachers and its quality research give it a competitive edge, especially in the job market.

Admission Tests

      While you search for degree programs on the University of Bologna website, you can see that there are two types of access to the programs:

  • Restricted access - entrance exam
  • Open access with assessment of personal competencies

      Many of the degree programs at Bologna University require candidates to take admission tests to verify their previous knowledge and background. Please take time to find out, if the program that you are applying to requires such test. If it does, take the admissions test first, then proceed to admissions.


Eligibility: Bachelor's and Combined Degree

      You can be admitted to a First Cycle or Single Cycle Degree Program if you hold a foreign secondary school diploma, which

  • was obtained following at least 12 years of schooling;
  • was obtained following a final period of at least two years of successful attendance in a foreign educational system;
  • grants access to a program at a university in one’s country of reference which is similar to that being applied to in Italy. If entrance exams are established for university admission in yourcountry (i.e. the Gao Kao in China) you must pass this academic test.

Eligibility: Master's Degree

      You can be admitted to a Second Cycle Degree Program if you hold a suitable foreign university or post-secondary qualification which allows study continuation in the subsequent cycle at a university in one’s country of reference.

      The suitability of the qualification for entry to Second Cycle Degree Programs at the University of Bologna is checked by the competent Degree Program Boards, which apply the same criteria (curricular requirements and assessment of personal competencies and skills) established for candidates holding Italian academic qualifications.

Admissions

      N.B: There is application fee of 50 euro in Bologna University.

      * before applying online, you will have to take the admission tests if your desired program requires it

      Step 1. Online application - website

      Fill up online application form and upload required documents.


      Step 2. Make pre-enrollment online and/or at the Italian Embassy.

      If you are a non-EU student residing abroad, in addition to applying for admission, you must pre-enroll through the Universitaly website.

      * Call and ask the Italian Embassy in your country if you will need to do the pre-enrollment online, or in-person at the Embassy.


      Step 3. Visa Interview and issuance


      Step 4. Complete your enrollment in Italy.

      If you are admitted, register on StudentiOnline and pay the first tuition instalment by the deadline set by your degree program, then follow the procedures to complete your enrollment at the University of Bologna.



Required documents

      Exact list of required documents can be found while filling the online application. However, general requirements are as follows:

  • Valid Passport or ID document

  • Proof of studies (12 years for Bachelor's degree and at least 3 years of university for Master's degree)

  • Transcript of records

  • SAT test (for some Bachelor's programs)

  • Language proficiency proof (at least B2: IELTS, CEFR, TOEFL, etc). Requirements vary according to the degree level and program



Deadlines

Scholarships

      Just like some other Italian regions, the region of Emilia Romagna also supports talented and promising students financially, by the local financial assistance program called "Er.Go". In order to be able to apply for regional scholarship a student is required to be admitted one of the Bachelor's or Master's programs mentioned above!

      The amount of scholarship goes from a maximum of € 5.397,74 to a minimum of € 1.097,55 and varies according to the economic conditions of the family unit and to the condition of non-resident student, commuter student and resident student.

      The scholarship will be paid in three annual installments. In December, in March, from June based on students` achieved credits on their academic performance. If they do not achieve required credits at university, they may use scholarship and in the worst case they have to give back the money they received in December.

      The best way to know more about Er.Go is to read its last year GUIDE.



Er.Go - How to apply?

      Step 1. Online application - Complete online application at http://www.er-go.it.

      Economic condition and required documents to fill online application.
  • Family unit composition at the moment of application (Your family is composed by all the family members living at the same house)
  • The previous year gross income of each member of your family unit, including brothers/sisters of legal age. If any does not have an income, is unemployed, a student, or a military, a declaration stating the amount of any unemployment benefit or other allowance received is needed; Number of months each family member worked in previous year (indicating whether it refers to a period of less or more than six months);
  • Floor area of the family home and of any other building owned by the family in the previous year. If your family does not have any property, you must submit a document issued by the competent authority certifying that no one in the family owns a real estate;
  • The amount of the eventual residual bank loan on 31/12 of the previous year on the family home and/or other properties.

      - The assets amount on 31/12 of the previous year for each family member of legal age.

      - The document certifying the student's invalidity or disability if student has disability.

      All income and asset values must be expressed in Euros according to the average exchange rate of the previous year, provided for by law. In any case, a currency converter is provided in the application form.

      Documents must be issued by the competent foreign Authority and translated into Italian, then legalized at the Italian Consulate/Embassy in your Country, (check their website in due time!) or apostilled, by the competent Authority.

      STEP 2. After coming to Italy, bring the originals of above documents to designated CAF office to obtain your ISEE Universitario.

      STEP 3. Submit the original documents and the ISEE Universitario to the Er.Go office.



Er.Go - deadlines

      Normally, online application opens at beginning of July and closes at the beginning of August for accommodation. For the 2020-2021 deadlines of the online application were

  • 8 July – 7 August, 2020 for accommodation;
  • 8 July – 4 September 2020 for scholarship.

      Deadlines for 2021-2022 academic year will be published in the middle of summer, check its website at that time. Until that time, it is suggested to prepare all required documents for the scholarship.
